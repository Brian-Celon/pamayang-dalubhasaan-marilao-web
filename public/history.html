Our History

Tracing the roots of academic excellence in Marilao, Bulacan.

The College Logo

The college logo bears symbols reflective of its humble beginnings and noble dreams. The outer yellow trimmings represent the sixteen barangays of the Municipality of Marilao whose residents will be the direct beneficiaries of the college.

The brown inner circle bears the name of the college; two stars representing the two initial curricular offerings and the year of its initial operation. At the center of the logo are three symbolic images: an open book with the municipal logo represents knowledge and wisdom which the municipality is determined to provide its people, guided by a torch of light and hope.

The four pillars that stand behind the open book represent the minds of four great men, who dreamt and worked for the realization of their dream.

Our Genesis

A Brighter Future for Every Marilenyo

In 2007, the would-be realization of a dream started from a simple conversation of four noble men: Mayor Epifanio V. Guillermo, Vice Mayor Juanito H. Santiago, Governor Joselito Mendoza and Mr. William R. Villarica: public servants, entrepreneurs, philanthropists; united in thoughts and inspired by a common vision.

Late 2007

The First Step: Setting the Direction

Mayor Peping Guillermo created the Technical Working Group tasked with conducting the FEASIBILITY of operating a local college, determining financial requirements, sustainability, market needs, and target beneficiaries. The result was not only positive, it was more than overwhelming.

2008

The Second Step: Legislative Authorization

The Sangguniang Bayan ng Marilao headed by Vice Mayor Juanito H. Santiago wasted no time. After three readings, Municipal Ordinance 671 was finally approved, and PDM was born.

February 2009

The Third Step: Building the Edifice

A piece of land donated by Mr. William Villarica, Atty. Henry & Mrs. Linabelle Villarica became the site. The modern design of the school's edifice became an inspiration. Donations poured in, and a groundbreaking ceremony was held in February 2009.

June 2009

The Fourth Step: Securing CHED Permit to Operate

The municipal government recognized the need for legitimization. Application for permit to operate commenced in consultation with the Commission on Higher Education (CHED). CHED appreciated how impressive the school was going to be, judging from its documentary compliance, physical facilities, initial faculty line-up, and library.

June 29, 2010

The Fifth Step: Getting Started to Operate

Inspired by CHED's comments, the municipal government mobilized all concerned offices to get the school ready. CHEDRO-III finally granted Government Permit to Operate (GP-094 for Hotel & Restaurant Management and GP-099 for Information Technology), covering academic years 2010-2011 and 2011-2012.

2010

Milestones & Firsts

April 26, 2010

First Day of Enrollment

PDM became an important landmark along MacArthur Highway as hundreds of students headed to the campus to be among its first enrollees.

May 5, 2010

Inauguration and Blessing

A historic date, as the entire people of Marilao implored GOD'S guidance and took a major leap as one people.

June 3, 2010

CHED's Final Visit

CHED Supervisors and the Regional Quality Assessment Team (RQAT) visited PDM to validate and certify readiness to operate.

June 16, 2010

The Very First Day of Classes

Classes officially began. The total number of the first batch of enrollees was 769 students.
